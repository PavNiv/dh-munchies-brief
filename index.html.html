Why EBITDA fell 57% while revenue grew

Before the levers — what is being attempted

The Current Attempt — Credit Before Critique

The launch of the 2-Minute Instant Veggie Idiyappam is a strategically sound instinct. The thinking behind it deserves to be acknowledged — because the intent is right. What follows shows where the execution has not yet caught up with the ambition.

What the launch is trying to do
1
Expand the usage occasion for idiyappam
Idiyappam is a weekend or occasional breakfast dish. The 2-minute format is a direct attempt to move it into the daily routine — a smart instinct given the frequency gap in the instant portfolio.
2
Enter the children's food space with a health angle
The veggie variants — beetroot, carrot, ragi — signal nutrition to mothers. The intent is to position this as a better-for-you alternative to Maggi in the school tiffin context.
3
Create trial urgency through a limited edition launch
The back-to-school timing and limited edition framing are designed to create FOMO and drive first purchase. The logic: scarcity forces trial before hesitation sets in.
4
Use colour variety as a differentiator
Four colour variants from real vegetable extracts is a genuine product innovation. The visual variety on the plate is distinctive and could command shelf attention.

Where the Execution Falls Short

Each intention above is valid. The issue is that execution has been wired to the wrong audience, wrong mechanic, and wrong occasion. Each gap below maps directly to a lever in the next section.

Expand to daily occasion
MISSED
The gap
Still positioned as a weekend breakfast product — the lowest frequency slot at 80 days a year. The school tiffin occasion at 200 days a year — 2.5× higher in volume — is not being targeted at all.

Health is the mother's language, not the child's. The child — the actual demand engine — has no voice in the product. No child pull means no repeat. The mother buys once out of curiosity and stops.
Drive trial through limited edition FOMO

FOMO without discovery is invisible. The product appears in only 1 of 5 relevant website categories. No homepage presence. No Kids / Tiffin collection. Trial cannot happen if the product cannot be found.

What comes next

The Growth Path

Three corrections, zero new product investment, and a phased scale roadmap. The first two actions start today and cost nothing.

A question worth exploring before the roadmap is drawn

A
Children's food brand — school tiffin, colour pull, mother as gatekeeper. 200 days × 160M children. Depth before breadth.
B
Instant food brand — convenience, quick commerce, working adults and teens. Different pack size, different channel, different message.
C
Both — but sequenced — start with children in Tamil Nadu and Kerala, build the habit base, then expand the occasion set. The most defensible path.
Why this question shapes everything

The product can serve all three directions — but the communication, pack design, pricing, and channel strategy are different for each. Right now, one undifferentiated approach speaks to none of them clearly. Choosing the primary target is the single decision that makes the roadmap obvious.
